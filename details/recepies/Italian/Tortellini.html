Tortellini

A classic Italian pasta variety known for its distinctive ring shape, traditionally stuffed with a flavorful filling. These small, ring-shaped pasta pieces are folded and sealed around a savory filling, typically made from ingredients such as cheese (like ricotta or Parmesan), meat (like prosciutto or ground beef), or vegetables (like spinach).

Ingredients

For The Pasta Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour, plus extra for dusting
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

For The Filling (example: ricotta and spinach):

  • 1 cup ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup cooked and drained spinach, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg yolk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: nutmeg or garlic powder for extra flavor

Equipement

  • Mixing bowls
  • Rolling pin or pasta machine
  • Knife or pizza cutter
  • Teaspoon
  • Fork or pastry brush
  • Large pot for boiling water

Preparing :

Preparing the Pasta Dough

  • On a clean surface or in a large mixing bowl, mound the flour and create a well in the center.
  • Crack the eggs into the well and add salt.
  • Using a fork, gradually mix the eggs into the flour until a dough forms.
  • Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. If the dough is too dry, add a little water; if too sticky, add a little flour.
  • Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for at least 30 minutes.

Preparing The Filling

  • In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese, chopped spinach, grated Parmesan cheese, egg yolk, salt, pepper, and any optional spices like nutmeg or garlic powder.
  • Mix until well combined. Adjust seasoning to taste.

Assembling The Tortellini

  • Roll out the Dough:

    • Divide the rested dough into smaller portions.

    Roll out each portion into a thin sheet, about 1/16 inch thick. You can use a rolling pin or a pasta machine for this step.

    Dust the dough with flour as needed to prevent sticking.

  • Cut and Shape the Tortellini:

    • Using a knife or a pizza cutter, cut the rolled-out dough into small squares, about 2 inches by 2 inches.

    Place a small teaspoonful of filling in the center of each square.

  • Form the Tortellini:

    • To shape the tortellini, fold each square diagonally to form a triangle, pressing the edges to seal.

    Take the two corners of the triangle opposite the folded edge and bring them together, overlapping slightly, to form a ring shape.

    Press the ends together firmly to seal. You can use a little water to help seal the edges if needed.

    Place the shaped tortellini on a floured surface or a lightly floured tray.

  • Cooking the Tortellini:

    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.

    Carefully drop the tortellini into the boiling water and cook for about 2-3 minutes, or until they float to the surface.

    Remove the cooked tortellini with a slotted spoon and transfer them to a serving dish.

Tips

  • Experiment with different fillings like meat, mushroom, or cheese variations.
  • Ensure the pasta dough is rolled thin enough to wrap around the filling without being too thick.
  • Keep the assembled tortellini covered with a damp cloth to prevent drying out while you work.
Enjoy your homemade tortellini as a delicious and satisfying Italian meal !
