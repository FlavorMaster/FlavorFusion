Brushetta

(pronounced "broo-SKET-uh") is an Italian appetizer that typically consists of toasted bread slices rubbed with garlic and topped with a mixture of diced tomatoes, basil, olive oil, and sometimes other ingredients like balsamic vinegar or cheese. It's a simple yet flavorful dish, often served as an antipasto or starter in Italian cuisine. The crispy bread contrasts nicely with the fresh and savory toppings, making it a popular choice for gatherings or as a light meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 loaf of crusty Italian bread or baguette
  • 3-4 ripe tomatoes, diced
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced or finely chopped
  • 6-8 fresh basil leaves, chopped (plus extra for garnish)
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Grated Parmesan cheese (optional, for garnish)

Preparing :

Prepare and Toast the Bread :

  • Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  • Slice the bread into 1/2-inch thick slices on a slight diagonal to increase surface area.
  • Place the bread slices on a baking sheet in a single layer.
  • Brush or drizzle olive oil over each slice.
  • Bake in the preheated oven for about 5-7 minutes, or until the bread slices are lightly golden and crisp. Watch them closely to avoid burning.

Prepare the Topping:

  • While the bread is toasting, prepare the topping.
  • In a bowl, combine the diced tomatoes, minced garlic, chopped basil leaves, olive oil, and balsamic vinegar (if using).
  • Season with salt and pepper to taste. Mix well to combine all the flavors.

Assemble the Bruschetta:

  • Once the bread slices are toasted, remove them from the oven and let them cool slightly.
  • Rub each slice with a peeled garlic clove for extra flavor (optional step).
  • Spoon a generous amount of the tomato mixture onto each slice of toasted bread, spreading it evenly.

Tips

  • Use ripe, flavorful tomatoes for the best taste.
  • Adjust the amount of garlic, basil, and other ingredients to suit your preference.
  • Bruschetta is best served fresh and eaten soon after assembling to enjoy the crunchiness of the bread.
Enjoy your homemade bruschetta as a delightful appetizer or light meal !
