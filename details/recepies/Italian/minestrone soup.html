Minestrone Soup

a hearty and flavorful Italian soup known for its rich variety of vegetables, beans, and pasta or rice. It's a versatile dish that can be adapted to use whatever fresh, seasonal vegetables are available, making it a popular and practical choice for home cooks.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 zucchini, diced
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can (15 oz) cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup small pasta (such as ditalini or macaroni)
  • 2 cups baby spinach or kale, chopped
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (optional)
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Preparing :

Sauté the Vegetables

  • Heat Oil :

    • In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat.

  • Add Aromatics:

    • Add the diced onion and sauté for about 5 minutes until it becomes translucent.

  • Add Garlic

    • Stir in the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.

  • Add Carrots and Celery:

    • Add the diced carrots and celery to the pot. Sauté for another 5-7 minutes until the vegetables start to soften.

  • Add Zucchini and Green Beans:

    • Stir in the diced zucchini and green beans. Cook for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Build the Soup

  • Add Tomatoes :

  • Pour in the can of diced tomatoes (including the juice) and stir to combine.

  • Add Broth

    • Pour in the vegetable broth, stirring well.

  • Season:

    • Add the dried oregano, basil, thyme, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine.

  • Bring to a Boil:

    • Increase the heat and bring the soup to a boil.

  • Simmer:

    • Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and let the soup simmer uncovered for about 20-25 minutes until the vegetables are tender.

  • Add Beans and Pasta

    • Stir in the cannellini beans.

    Add the small pasta to the pot. Continue to simmer for another 10-12 minutes until the pasta is al dente.

  • Add greens

    • Stir in the chopped spinach or kale. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the greens are wilted.

  • Adjust Seasoning:

    • Taste the soup and adjust the seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed.

Tips

  • Vegetable Variety: Feel free to use other vegetables like potatoes, peas, or corn based on your preference or availability.
  • Broth: For a richer flavor, you can use a mix of vegetable broth and chicken broth if you’re not vegetarian.
  • Pasta: Cook the pasta separately and add it to each bowl before serving if you plan to have leftovers. This prevents the pasta from becoming mushy.
Enjoy your delicious homemade minestrone soup!
