a classic Italian pizza that features a simple yet delicious combination of ingredients: a thin crust topped with tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella cheese, and fresh basil leaves. It's typically drizzled with a bit of olive oil and sometimes sprinkled with salt. The Margherita pizza is known for its vibrant colors, representing the Italian flag—red (tomato), white (mozzarella), and green (basil). This pizza is beloved for its fresh, authentic flavors and minimalist approach, showcasing the quality of its ingredients.

Marguerita

Ingredients

For the Dough:

  • 2 1/4 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (110°F/45°C)
  • 3 1/2 to 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

For the Topping:

  • 1 cup crushed tomatoes (San Marzano tomatoes are ideal)
  • 8 ounces fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra-virgin olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Preparing :

Making the Dough

  • Activate the Yeast:

    • In a small bowl, combine the yeast and warm water. Stir gently to dissolve the yeast.

    Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.

  • Mix the Dough:

    • In a large mixing bowl, combine 3 1/2 cups of flour and 2 teaspoons of salt.

    Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture and 2 tablespoons of olive oil.

    Stir with a wooden spoon until the dough starts to come together.

  • Knead the Dough:

    • Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface.

    Knead for about 10 minutes, adding more flour as needed, until the dough is smooth and elastic.

  • First Rise:

    • Lightly oil a large bowl and place the dough in it, turning to coat the dough with oil.

    Cover with a damp cloth or plastic wrap.

    Let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours, or until doubled in size.

Preparing the Pizza:

  • Preheat the Oven:

    • Place a pizza stone or an inverted baking sheet in the oven.

    Preheat the oven to its highest setting, typically around 500°F (260°C).

  • Shape the Dough:

    • Punch down the dough to release the air.

    Divide it into 2 equal portions if making two pizzas.

    On a lightly floured surface, stretch and shape each portion into a 12-inch round. Be careful not to overwork the dough.

  • Prepare the Toppings

    • Spread a thin layer of crushed tomatoes over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.

    Arrange the mozzarella slices evenly over the tomatoes.

    Scatter fresh basil leaves on top.

Baking

  • Drizzle a little extra-virgin olive oil over the pizza.
  • Carefully transfer the pizza to the preheated pizza stone or baking sheet.
  • Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.

Tips

  • Use Quality Ingredients: Choose San Marzano tomatoes, fresh mozzarella, and fresh basil.
  • Preheat Oven and Stone: Set your oven to its highest temperature and preheat a pizza stone or baking sheet.
  • Gently Stretch Dough: Use your hands to stretch the dough gently, and let it rest if it resists.
Enjoy your homemade Margherita pizza!
