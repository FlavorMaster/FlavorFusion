Seafood Gnocchi

gnocchi is a luxurious Italian dish that combines tender potato dumplings (gnocchi) with a variety of seafood, typically in a creamy sauce. The gnocchi, made from mashed potatoes and flour, provide a soft and pillowy base that complements the delicate flavors of shrimp, scallops, crab meat, or other seafood choices. The dish is often enhanced with a rich and velvety cream sauce, infused with garlic, Parmesan cheese, and fresh herbs like parsley. Seafood gnocchi is celebrated for its harmonious blend of textures and flavors, offering a satisfying and elegant dining experience reminiscent of coastal Italian cuisine.

Ingredients

For The Gnocchi

  • 2 pounds (about 4 large) russet potatoes, baked or boiled until tender
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 1/2 - 2 cups all-purpose flour, plus extra for dusting
  • Salt, to taste

For The Seafood and Sauce

  • 1 pound mixed seafood (such as shrimp, scallops, and/or crab meat), peeled and deveined if needed
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: lemon zest or white wine for additional flavor

Equipement

  • Large pot for boiling potatoes
  • Potato ricer or masher
  • Large pot for cooking gnocchi
  • Large skillet or saucepan
  • Slotted spoon
  • Mixing bowls

Preparing :

Preparing the Gnocchi:

  • Prepare the Potatoes:

    • Pierce the potatoes with a fork and bake them in the oven at 400°F (200°C) for about 45-60 minutes, or boil them in water until tender. Let them cool slightly.

  • Make the Gnocchi Dough:

    • Once cooled, peel the potatoes and either pass them through a potato ricer or mash them until smooth.

    Spread the mashed potatoes on a clean surface or in a large bowl. Make a well in the center and add the beaten egg and salt.

  • Form the Dough:

    • Gradually add the flour, mixing with your hands, until the dough comes together and is soft but not sticky. Be careful not to overwork the dough.

  • Shape the Gnocchi:

    • On a lightly floured surface, divide the dough into smaller portions.

    Roll each portion into a long rope about 1/2 inch thick.

    Cut the rope into bite-sized pieces, about 1 inch long. You can use a knife or a bench scraper for this.

  • Cook the Gnocchi:

    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.

    Carefully drop the gnocchi into the boiling water in batches. Cook until they float to the surface, about 2-3 minutes. Use a slotted spoon to transfer them to a plate or tray.

Making the Seafood and Sauce:

  • Cook the Seafood

    • In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add the mixed seafood and cook until just done, about 3-5 minutes. Remove the seafood from the skillet and set aside.

  • Make the Sauce:

    • In the same skillet, add the white wine and cook for 2-3 minutes until reduced by half. Add the fish or vegetable stock and bring to a simmer. Stir in the heavy cream and Parmesan cheese, cooking until the sauce thickens slightly, about 5 minutes.

  • Add the Tomatoes:

    • Stir in the cherry tomatoes and cook until they soften, about 2-3 minutes. Season the sauce with salt and pepper to taste.

  • Combine with Seafood:

    • Return the cooked seafood to the skillet and mix gently to combine.

Combine and Serve

  • Add the Gnocchi:

    • Add the cooked gnocchi to the skillet with the seafood sauce. Toss gently to coat the gnocchi in the sauce.

  • Garnish and Serve

    • Garnish with fresh parsley and serve immediately. Enjoy your homemade seafood gnocchi!

Tips

  • Use starchy potatoes like Russets or Yukon Golds. They have less moisture and will yield lighter gnocchi.
  • After boiling, let the potatoes cool and dry out a bit before mashing. This helps prevent the dough from becoming too sticky.
  • Knead the dough just until it comes together. Overworking the dough can make the gnocchi tough.
Feel free to adjust the ingredients and seasonings to your taste. Enjoy your meal!
