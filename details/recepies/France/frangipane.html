FRANGIPANE TART

a classic French pastry featuring a sweet almond filling known as frangipane, made from ground almonds, butter, sugar, and eggs. The tart typically has a buttery, flaky crust that holds the rich and creamy frangipane filling. Often, it's topped with sliced fruits like pears, apples, or berries, which add a delicious contrast to the nutty sweetness of the filling. The tart is baked until golden brown and can be served warm or at room temperature, often dusted with powdered sugar or glazed with apricot jam for added shine.

Ingredients

For the Tart Crust (Pâte Sucrée):

  • 1 1/4 cups (160g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup (30g) powdered sugar
  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small cubes
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1-2 tablespoons cold water

For the Frangipane Filling:

  • 1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar
  • 1 cup (100g) almond flour (finely ground almonds)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon almond extract
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour

For the Topping:

  • Fresh fruits (e.g., pears, apples, berries, or apricots), sliced
  • Apricot jam (optional, for glazing)

Preparing :

Making the Tart Crust

  • Prepare the Dough:

    • In a food processor, combine the flour and powdered sugar.

    Add the chilled butter cubes and pulse until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.

    Add the egg yolk and pulse a few times.

    Add cold water, one tablespoon at a time, and pulse until the dough starts to come together.

  • Chill the Dough:

    • Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gather it into a ball.

    Flatten it into a disk, wrap it in plastic wrap, and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  • Roll and Fit the Dough:

    • Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).

    On a lightly floured surface, roll out the chilled dough to fit a 9-inch tart pan with a removable bottom.

    Press the dough into the pan and trim the edges.

    Prick the bottom of the crust with a fork and place it in the freezer for about 10 minutes.

  • Blind Bake the Crust:

    • Line the crust with parchment paper and fill it with pie weights or dried beans.

    Bake for 15 minutes, then remove the weights and parchment paper.

    Bake for an additional 5-7 minutes, until the crust is lightly golden. Set aside to cool.

Making the Frangipane Filling:

  • Prepare the Frangipane:

    • In a medium bowl, cream together the softened butter and granulated sugar until light and fluffy.

    Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition.

    Mix in the almond extract.

    Stir in the almond flour and all-purpose flour until well combined.

Assembling and Baking the Tart:

  • Fill the Tart:

    • Spread the frangipane filling evenly in the pre-baked tart crust.

  • Arrange the Fruit:

    • Arrange the Fruit:

  • Bake the Tart:

    • Bake in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the frangipane filling is set and golden brown.

  • Glaze the Tart (Optional):

    • If desired, heat a few tablespoons of apricot jam in a small saucepan until melted.

    Brush the melted jam over the top of the tart to give it a glossy finish.

  • Cool and Serve:

    • Allow the tart to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then remove it from the pan and let it cool completely on a wire rack.

    Slice and serve at room temperature.

Tips

  • Chill the Dough: Chilling the dough before rolling and baking helps prevent shrinkage
  • Fresh Fruit Choices: Use fresh, ripe fruits for the best flavor and presentation.
  • Even Filling: Spread the frangipane filling evenly to ensure it bakes uniformly.
Enjoy your delightful Frangipane Tart, a perfect blend of buttery crust, creamy almond filling, and fresh fruits!
