BOEUF BOURGUIGNON

A traditional French stew made with beef braised in red wine, typically Burgundy, along with beef broth. The dish is flavored with garlic, onions, and mushrooms, and often includes carrots and pearl onions. Slow-cooked to tender perfection, it is rich and hearty, with a deep, complex flavor enhanced by the wine and aromatic vegetables. It's typically served with potatoes, noodles, or crusty bread to soak up the savory sauce.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/2 lbs (1.2 kg) beef chuck, cut into 2-inch cubes
  • Salt and pepper
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 8 oz (225 g) bacon, diced
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 3 cups red wine (preferably Burgundy)
  • 2-3 cups beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 bouquet garni (thyme, bay leaf, and parsley tied together)
  • 8 oz (225 g) pearl onions, peeled
  • 8 oz (225 g) mushrooms, quartered
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Preparing :

  • Preheat the Oven:

    • Preheat the Oven:

  • Season the Beef:

    • Pat the beef cubes dry with paper towels.

    Season them generously with salt and pepper.

Cooking

  • Brown the Beef:

    • In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium-high heat.

    Add the beef cubes in batches, searing them until browned on all sides. Do not overcrowd the pot.

    Remove the browned beef and set aside.

  • Cook the Bacon:

    • In the same pot, add the diced bacon and cook until crispy.

    Remove the bacon with a slotted spoon and set aside.

  • Sauté Vegetables:

    • Add the sliced onion and carrots to the pot with the bacon fat.

    Cook until the onions are lightly browned, about 5 minutes.

  • Combine Beef and Bacon with Vegetables:

    • Return the beef and bacon to the pot with the sautéed vegetables.

    Sprinkle the flour over the mixture and toss to coat the beef evenly.

    Place the pot in the preheated oven uncovered for 4 minutes.

    Stir the mixture and return to the oven for another 4 minutes. This helps to form a crust.

  • Deglaze and Simmer:

    • Remove the pot from the oven and reduce the oven temperature to 325°F (160°C).

    Add the wine, beef broth (enough to barely cover the meat), tomato paste, garlic, and bouquet garni.

    Bring to a simmer on the stovetop.

    Cover the pot and place it in the oven for 2 1/2 to 3 hours, until the meat is very tender.

Finishing Touches:

  • Prepare the Garnish:

    • While the stew is cooking, prepare the pearl onions and mushrooms.

    In a separate pan, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil and sauté the pearl onions until browned and tender.

    In the same pan, sauté the quartered mushrooms until browned.

  • Combine and Simmer:

    • Once the meat is tender, remove the pot from the oven.

    Add the sautéed pearl onions and mushrooms to the stew.

    Simmer on the stovetop for 15 minutes to blend the flavors.

  • Adjust Seasoning

    • Remove the bouquet garni.

    Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.

    Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

Tips

  • Use Quality Wine: Choose a good-quality Burgundy or Pinot Noir wine for the best flavor.
  • Sear in Batches: Sear the beef in small batches to ensure it browns properly and doesn’t steam.
  • Rest Overnight: For even better flavor, let the stew rest overnight and reheat it gently the next day.
Enjoy your rich and flavorful Boeuf Bourguignon with crusty bread, mashed potatoes, or a side of buttered noodles!
