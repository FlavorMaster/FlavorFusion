HAZELNUT DARCQOISE

a classic French dessert consisting of layers of hazelnut meringue, typically filled with rich buttercream, ganache, or whipped cream. The meringue layers are made from egg whites, sugar, and finely ground hazelnuts, baked until crisp and golden. Once assembled, the dessert is often garnished with additional hazelnuts, chocolate shavings, or powdered sugar. The combination of the crunchy meringue and creamy filling creates a delightful texture and a rich, nutty flavor that is both elegant and indulgent.

Ingredients

For the Hazelnut Meringue:

  • 1 cup (120g) hazelnut flour (finely ground hazelnuts)
  • 1 cup (120g) powdered sugar
  • 6 large egg whites, at room temperature
  • 1/2 cup (100g) granulated sugar

For the Hazelnut Praline Buttercream:

  • 1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
  • 2/3 cup (160ml) water
  • 4 large egg yolks
  • 1 cup (230g) unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup (100g) hazelnut praline paste

For Assembly

  • 1/2 cup (60g) chopped toasted hazelnuts
  • Powdered sugar for dusting

Preparing :

Making the Hazelnut Meringue:

  • Prepare the Baking Sheets:

    • Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).

    Line two baking sheets with parchment paper. Draw three 8-inch circles (or the size you prefer) on the parchment paper to use as a guide.

  • Mix Dry Ingredients:

    • In a bowl, sift together the hazelnut flour and powdered sugar.

  • Whip Egg Whites:

    • In a clean mixing bowl, beat the egg whites until they form soft peaks.

    Gradually add the granulated sugar and continue to beat until the egg whites are stiff and glossy.

  • Fold in Dry Ingredients:

    • Gently fold the hazelnut flour and powdered sugar mixture into the beaten egg whites until well combined.

  • Pipe or Spread Meringue:

    • Transfer the meringue mixture to a piping bag fitted with a plain round tip.

    Pipe or spread the meringue into the drawn circles on the parchment paper, smoothing the tops with a spatula.

  • Bake the Meringue:

    • Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the meringue is crisp and lightly golden.

    Let the meringue layers cool completely on the baking sheets.

Making the Hazelnut Praline Buttercream:

  • Make Sugar Syrup:

    • In a small saucepan, combine the granulated sugar and water.

    Cook over medium heat until the sugar dissolves and the mixture reaches the soft ball stage (240°F/115°C) on a candy thermometer.

  • Beat Egg Yolks:

    • In a mixing bowl, beat the egg yolks until thick and pale.

  • Combine Syrup and Yolks:

    • Slowly pour the hot sugar syrup into the beaten egg yolks while continuously whisking.

    Slowly pour the hot sugar syrup into the beaten egg yolks while continuously whisking.

  • Add Butter and Praline Paste:

    • Gradually add the softened butter to the egg yolk mixture, beating until smooth and fluffy.

    Mix in the hazelnut praline paste until well combined.

Assembling the Dacquoise:

  • Assembling the Dacquoise:

    • Place one meringue layer on a serving plate.

    Spread a third of the hazelnut praline buttercream over the meringue.

    Repeat with the remaining meringue layers and buttercream, finishing with a layer of buttercream on top.

  • Garnish and Serve:

    • Sprinkle the top layer with chopped toasted hazelnuts.

    Dust with powdered sugar for an elegant finish.

  • Chill Before Serving:

    • Refrigerate the assembled Dacquoise for at least 2 hours before serving to allow the flavors to meld and the layers to set.

Tips

  • Hazelnut Flour: If you can't find hazelnut flour, you can make your own by grinding toasted hazelnuts in a food processor until fine
  • Stable Meringue: Ensure your mixing bowl and beaters are clean and dry when whipping egg whites to achieve a stable meringue.
  • Praline Paste: You can make hazelnut praline paste by blending caramelized hazelnuts into a smooth paste, or you can purchase it from specialty stores.
Enjoy your delicate and delicious Hazelnut Dacquoise!
