Chicken Confit

a French dish where chicken legs are slowly cooked in their own fat until tender and flavorful. The process involves curing the chicken with salt, garlic, and herbs (such as thyme and bay leaves) for several hours or overnight, then cooking it gently in rendered chicken fat at a low temperature. The result is incredibly moist, flavorful, and tender meat with a crispy skin when finished in the oven. Chicken confit can be served with roasted vegetables, potatoes, or a fresh salad.

Ingredients

  • 4 chicken legs (thighs and drumsticks)
  • 1/4 cup kosher salt
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 teaspoons black peppercorns
  • 2 teaspoons black peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon fresh thyme leaves or 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 4 cups rendered chicken fat, duck fat, or a combination of fat and olive oil

Preparing :

  • Cure the Chicken:

    • Rub the chicken legs with kosher salt, minced garlic, black peppercorns, crumbled bay leaves, and thyme.

    Place the chicken in a dish, cover with plastic wrap, and refrigerate for 24 hours to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat.

Cooking

  • Preheat the Oven:

    • Preheat your oven to 225°F (110°C).

  • Rinse and Dry the Chicken:

    • After curing, rinse the chicken legs under cold water to remove excess salt and herbs.

    Pat the chicken legs dry with paper towels.

  • Arrange in a Baking Dish:

    • Place the chicken legs in a single layer in a heavy, ovenproof dish or Dutch oven.

    Pour the rendered fat over the chicken legs, ensuring they are fully submerged.

  • Slow Cook the Chicken:

    • Cover the dish with a lid or aluminum foil.

    Place in the preheated oven and cook for 2 1/2 to 3 hours, until the chicken is tender and the meat easily pulls away from the bone.

  • Crisp the Skin (Optional):

    • To serve immediately, remove the chicken legs from the fat and let excess fat drip off.

    Heat a skillet over medium-high heat and place the chicken legs skin-side down until the skin is crispy and golden brown, about 5-7 minutes.

  • Storage (Optional):

    • If not serving immediately, let the chicken cool in the fat to room temperature, then refrigerate. The chicken can be stored in the fat for up to a week.

  • Reheat and Serve:

    • When ready to serve, gently reheat the chicken legs in a 350°F (175°C) oven for 15-20 minutes, or until warmed through.

    Serve with your choice of sides, such as roasted vegetables, potatoes, or a fresh salad.

Tips

  • Use Good-Quality Fat: Rendered duck fat or chicken fat is traditional and adds rich flavor.
  • Slow and Low Cooking: Maintain a low oven temperature for slow cooking
  • Crisp Before Serving: For a perfect finish, crisp the skin in a skillet just before serving
Enjoy your rich and flavorful Chicken Confit!
