BOUILLABAISSSE

A traditional Provençal fish stew originating from the port city of Marseille, France. It features a variety of Mediterranean fish and shellfish, simmered with aromatic herbs, fennel, tomatoes, leeks, onions, and garlic. Saffron and orange zest add distinctive flavors. The broth is rich and flavorful, often served with a side of rouille (a garlicky mayonnaise) and crusty bread. This hearty, aromatic stew embodies the essence of coastal French cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (900 g) mixed fish and shellfish (such as cod, halibut, shrimp, mussels, and clams)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 leek, white and light green parts only, sliced
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 fennel bulb, thinly sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 orange or yellow bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1 can (14 oz / 400 g) diced tomatoes, with juices
  • 4 cups fish or seafood broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish
  • Crusty bread for serving

Preparing :

  • Prepare the Seafood:

    • Clean and scrub the shellfish (mussels and clams).

    Peel and devein the shrimp, if needed.

    Cut the fish into bite-sized chunks.

Cooking

  • Sauté Aromatics:

    • In a large Dutch oven or soup pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat.

    Add the chopped onion, sliced leek, minced garlic, thinly sliced fennel bulb, and chopped bell peppers.

    Sauté until the vegetables are softened, about 5-7 minutes.

  • Add Spices and Herbs:

    • Stir in the saffron threads, bay leaf, dried thyme, dried oregano, dried basil, and cayenne pepper (if using).

    Cook for 1 minute until fragrant.

  • Deglaze with Wine:

    • Pour in the dry white wine and scrape any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.

    Let the wine reduce for 2-3 minutes.

  • Simmer with Tomatoes and Broth:

    • Add the diced tomatoes with their juices and fish or seafood broth to the pot.

    Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and let it simmer for 15-20 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.

  • Cook Seafood:

    • Add the fish chunks and shrimp to the pot.

    Simmer gently for 5-7 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and the shrimp turn pink.

  • Add Shellfish:

    • Add the cleaned mussels and clams to the pot.

    Cover and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the shellfish have opened. Discard any that do not open.

  • Adjust Seasoning

    • Taste and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

    Ladle the Bouillabaisse into bowls, making sure to distribute the seafood and vegetables evenly.

    Garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro.

  • Serve with Crusty Bread:

    • Serve immediately with slices of crusty bread for dipping into the flavorful broth.

Tips

  • Variety of Seafood: Use a variety of seafood for depth of flavor.
  • Saffron Infusion: Steep the saffron threads in a tablespoon of warm water before adding to the pot.
  • Serve Fresh: Bouillabaisse is best served fresh and hot.
Enjoy your homemade Bouillabaisse, a delightful taste of the Mediterranean seafood stew!
