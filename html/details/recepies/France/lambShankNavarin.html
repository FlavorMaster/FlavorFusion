LAMB SHANK NAVARIN

A classic French stew made with lamb shanks and a variety of spring vegetables. The dish gets its name from "navet," the French word for turnip, which is a key ingredient. The lamb shanks are browned and then slow-cooked in a rich broth of wine, stock, tomatoes, and aromatics such as garlic and herbs. The stew also includes vegetables like carrots, potatoes, and peas. The slow cooking process makes the lamb tender and infuses the dish with deep, savory flavors. Lamb Shank Navarin is typically served with crusty bread or over a bed of mashed potatoes or rice.

Ingredients

  • 4 lamb shanks
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 cups red wine
  • 4 cups beef or chicken broth
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
  • 2 cups small new potatoes, halved
  • 1 cup pearl onions, peeled
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed
  • 1 cup peas (fresh or frozen)
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Preparing :

  • Season and Brown the Lamb Shanks:

    • Season the lamb shanks with salt and pepper.

    In a large Dutch oven, heat the olive oil over medium-high heat.

    Brown the lamb shanks on all sides, about 10 minutes. Remove the shanks and set aside.

  • Sauté the Vegetables:

    • In the same pot, add the chopped onion, carrots, and celery.

    Cook until the vegetables are softened, about 5-7 minutes.

    Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute.

  • Add Tomato Paste and Wine:

    • Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes.

    Pour in the red wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.

    Bring to a simmer and cook until the wine is reduced by half, about 5 minutes.

  • Add Broth and Tomatoes:

    • Add the beef or chicken broth, diced tomatoes, bay leaves, thyme, and rosemary.

    Return the lamb shanks to the pot, ensuring they are submerged in the liquid.

Cooking

  • Simmer the Stew:

    • Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low.

    Cover and simmer for about 2 hours, or until the lamb shanks are tender and the meat is falling off the bone.

  • Add Potatoes and Onions:

    • Add the halved new potatoes and pearl onions to the pot.

    Continue to simmer for another 30 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender.

  • Add Green Beans and Peas:

    • Add the green beans and peas to the pot.

    Cook for an additional 10 minutes, until the vegetables are tender but still vibrant.

  • Adjust Seasoning:

    • Taste the stew and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

  • Garnish and Serve:

    • Garnish with fresh parsley before serving.

    Serve the Lamb Shank Navarin hot, with crusty bread or over a bed of mashed potatoes if desired.

Tips

  • Low and Slow Cooking: Cook the stew over low heat for a long time to ensure the lamb shanks become tender and flavorful.
  • Wine Choice: Use a good-quality red wine for cooking, such as a Pinot Noir or Cabernet Sauvignon, to add depth to the stew.
  • Vegetable Timing: Add vegetables at different stages to ensure they are perfectly cooked and retain their texture.
Enjoy your rich and flavorful Lamb Shank Navarin!
