CROQUE MONSIEUR

a classic French ham and cheese sandwich, typically made with ham, Gruyère or Emmental cheese, and béchamel sauce, all layered between slices of buttered bread. The sandwich is then topped with more cheese and either broiled or baked until the cheese is melted and golden brown. It is a rich and indulgent dish, often enjoyed as a café or bistro staple. When topped with a fried or poached egg, it becomes a Croque Madame.

Ingredients

  • 8 slices of white bread (or your preferred bread)
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, softened
  • 4 teaspoons Dijon mustard
  • 8 thin slices of ham
  • 2 cups grated Gruyère or Emmental cheese
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Pinch of nutmeg (optional)

Preparing :

Prepare the Béchamel Sauce:

  • In a small saucepan, melt 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat.
  • Add the flour and whisk continuously for about 2 minutes, until it forms a smooth paste (roux).
  • Gradually add the milk, whisking constantly to avoid lumps.
  • Cook until the sauce thickens and is smooth, about 4-5 minutes.
  • Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg (if using).
  • Remove from heat and set aside.

Assembling the Sandwiches:

  • Preheat the Oven:

    • Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).

  • Prepare the Bread:

    • Lightly butter one side of each bread slice.

    Place the bread slices, buttered side down, on a baking sheet.

  • Assemble the Sandwiches:

    • Spread 1 teaspoon of Dijon mustard on the unbuttered side of half the bread slices.

    Layer 2 slices of ham on top of each slice with mustard.

    Sprinkle half of the grated cheese over the ham.

    Top each sandwich with the remaining bread slices, buttered side up.

  • Top with Béchamel and Cheese:

    • Spoon the béchamel sauce evenly over the top of each sandwich.

    Sprinkle the remaining grated cheese over the béchamel sauce.

Cooking

  • Bake the Sandwiches:

    • Place the baking sheet in the preheated oven.

    Bake for 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and golden brown.

    For extra crispiness, you can broil the sandwiches for an additional 1-2 minutes, watching closely to prevent burning.

  • Serve Hot:

    • Remove the sandwiches from the oven and let them cool slightly before serving.

    Serve immediately, optionally with a simple green salad on the side.

Tips

  • Use Quality Cheese: Gruyère or Emmental cheese gives the best flavor .
  • Béchamel Consistency: Ensure your béchamel sauce is smooth and thick enough.
  • Crispy Finish: For a crispier texture, briefly broil the sandwiches after baking.
Enjoy your delicious and classic Croque Monsieur!
