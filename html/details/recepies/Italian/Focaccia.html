Focaccia

A traditional Italian flatbread known for its soft, airy texture and golden, crispy crust. Typically seasoned with olive oil, salt, and fresh herbs, it can be topped with various ingredients like garlic, tomatoes, olives, or cheese. Often enjoyed as an appetizer, side dish, or sandwich base, focaccia is beloved for its versatility and rich flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon honey
  • 1 1/4 cups warm water (110°F/45°C)
  • 3 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for drizzling
  • 2 teaspoons sea salt
  • Fresh rosemary, coarse sea salt, and other toppings (optional)

Preparing :

Activate the Yeast

  • In a small bowl, combine the yeast, honey, and warm water. Stir gently to dissolve the yeast and honey.
  • Let the mixture sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy. This indicates that the yeast is active.

Mix and Knead the Dough:

  • In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and 2 teaspoons of sea salt.
  • Create a well in the center and add the yeast mixture and 1/4 cup of olive oil.
  • Stir with a wooden spoon or spatula until the dough begins to come together.
  • Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface.
  • Knead for about 10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic. If the dough is too sticky, add a little more flour as needed.

First Rise:

  • Lightly oil a large bowl and place the dough in it, turning the dough to coat it with oil.
  • Cover the bowl with a damp cloth or plastic wrap.
  • Let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours, or until doubled in size.

Shape the Dough:

  • Punch down the dough to release the air.
  • Transfer the dough to a lightly oiled baking sheet or a 9x13 inch pan.
  • Using your hands, gently stretch and press the dough to fit the pan. If the dough resists stretching, let it rest for a few minutes and then try again.

Second Rise:

  • Cover the dough with a damp cloth or plastic wrap.
  • Let it rise again in a warm place for about 20-30 minutes.

Prepare for Baking:

  • Preheat your oven to 450°F (230°C).
  • Use your fingers to make deep dimples all over the surface of the dough.
  • Drizzle the top generously with olive oil.
  • Sprinkle with fresh rosemary, coarse sea salt, and any other desired toppings (e.g., cherry tomatoes, olives, garlic).

Baking

  • Bake the focaccia in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and the bread sounds hollow when tapped.

Tips

  • Knead the dough until it’s smooth and elastic. This helps develop the gluten, which gives the bread its structure.
  • Allow the dough to rise in a warm, draft-free area.
  • Don’t be afraid to press your fingers deeply into the dough to create dimples.
Enjoy your homemade focaccia!
