Ravioli with Ricotta

a classic Italian pasta dish consisting of delicate pasta parcels filled with creamy ricotta cheese. The ravioli are typically served with a simple sauce, such as a light tomato sauce or sage butter, allowing the flavor of the ricotta to shine through. Garnishes like grated Parmesan cheese or fresh herbs add a finishing touch to this comforting and flavorful dish.

Ingredients

For the Pasta Dough:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Pinch of salt

For the Filling:

  • 1 1/2 cups ricotta cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg yolk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: Nutmeg or herbs like parsley or basil, finely chopped

For the Sauce (Optional):

  • Simple tomato sauce or sage butter for serving
  • Additional grated Parmesan cheese for garnish

Preparing :

Making the Pasta Dough:

  • Prepare the Dough:

    • On a clean work surface, mound the flour and make a well in the center.

    Crack the eggs into the well, add olive oil, and a pinch of salt.

    Using a fork, gradually beat the eggs and incorporate the flour from the edges until a dough forms.

  • Knead the Dough:

    • Knead the dough for about 8-10 minutes until smooth and elastic. If it's too sticky, add a little more flour; if too dry, add a few drops of water.

    Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest at room temperature for at least 30 minutes.

Making the Filling:

  • Prepare the Filling:

    • In a mixing bowl, combine ricotta cheese, grated Parmesan cheese, egg yolk, salt, pepper, and any optional ingredients like nutmeg or herbs.

    Mix until well combined and smooth. Adjust seasoning to taste.

Assembling The Ravioli

  • Roll Out the Dough:

    • Divide the rested dough into 2 portions. Keep one portion covered with a towel or plastic wrap to prevent drying out.

    On a lightly floured surface, roll out one portion of dough into a thin sheet, about 1/16 to 1/8 inch thick. Ensure it's evenly rolled out.

  • Fill and Shape the Ravioli:

    • Spoon teaspoon-sized amounts of filling onto the dough sheet, leaving space between each mound.

    Lightly brush water around each mound of filling to help seal the ravioli.

    Carefully place another rolled-out sheet of dough on top.

    Press around each mound of filling to seal the edges, ensuring there are no air pockets.

  • Cut and Seal:

    • Use a ravioli cutter or a sharp knife to cut out individual ravioli squares or circles. Press the edges firmly to seal each ravioli.

Baking

  • Cooking the Ravioli:

    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.

    Carefully drop the ravioli into the boiling water, a few at a time, stirring gently to prevent sticking.

    Cook for about 2-3 minutes, or until the ravioli float to the surface and are cooked through.

Tips

  • Avoid overfilling the ravioli to prevent them from bursting during cooking.
  • Use a brush with water to seal the edges of the ravioli firmly.
  • Press out any air pockets to ensure the ravioli hold their shape when cooked.
Enjoy your homemade ravioli with ricotta!
